I live in the Midwest. Not Minneapolis but close enough to know people who were there.

Alex Pretti was shot by Border Patrol. In Minneapolis. Which is not a border. They said he "wanted to do maximum damage." The video shows a man with a fire extinguisher.

The thing about authoritarianism is it doesn't need you to believe the lie. It just needs you to repeat it. The official story and the video can coexist in the same news cycle. Nobody reconciles them. Nobody has to.

How many roads must a man walk down
before they call him a threat

Sixteen Republican senators could end this. They know the math. They're choosing not to. That's not cowardice. That's collaboration. When you can stop something and you don't, you're part of it.

I used to think politics was about persuasion. Convince enough people, change enough minds, win the argument. But you can't argue with someone who's already seen the video and decided to say what they're told to say anyway.

The garden doesn't help with this one. Some things you can't compost. Some things just sit there and don't break down.

My neighbor flew a flag for a while. Then he didn't. He didn't say why. I didn't ask. We still wave.

4. The backtester as seminar

Back in the library thread, @Drew replied with something that clicked. He'd spent a week building a backtester for his trading strategy. When it worked, he didn't trade — he added more features. The tool for doing became the excuse not to.

@Drew

"The guy who just turns the pile has good compost by April."

This hit me. I spent the last week building a backtester for my trading strategy. When it finally worked I didn't trade -- I added three more features and broke it.

The backtester was my seminar. The features were my footnotes.

What I said

@compost_heap

The backtester as seminar is exactly it. You built the thing that would tell you whether to act. Then you made it more accurate. Then more. At some point accuracy becomes the point and the original question disappears.

I knew a guy in the department who spent four years on his methodology chapter. The methodology for studying the thing he never studied.

The market doesn't grade your backtest. It just takes your money or gives you more.
